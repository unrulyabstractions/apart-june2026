%% ABSTRACT

\begin{abstract}
On ambiguous Spanish \textsc{SESGO} questions naming no specific person the
correct answer is \emph{unknown}; on disambiguated ones a specific role is
correct. We test a debiasing \emph{scaffold}---a one-sentence preamble prepended
to the question to induce abstention---using a five-study framework that scores
every item under three readouts (a teacher-forced three-option distribution over
\{target, other, unknown\}, a two-option forced choice, and a greedy reasoning
decode) against a per-condition gold. On \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} ($n{=}2310$) the
model abstains at 71--87\% under three-option forcing but reaches 28--46\% on
disambiguated items. The selected scaffold $s^\star$ is the no-scaffold baseline,
which abstains on 80\% of ambiguous items ($n{=}35$) under three-option forcing
and 86\% under thinking ($n{=}22$). A five-model Qwen/Llama/Mistral sweep ($n{=}770$/cell) shows
abstention and disambiguated accuracy rise with scale. Probing answer-position
activations ($n{=}462$, scaffold vs.\ none), the debiasing scaffold is the most
separable axis ($+0.48$ silhouette) while social identity collapses ($-0.48$). We
release the harness.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem}

On an \emph{ambiguous} \textsc{SESGO} question---one whose text names no specific
person---the correct answer is \emph{unknown}. A model that instead commits to a
specific social group (the \emph{target} or the \emph{other} group) is biased. On
a \emph{disambiguated} question the text names a person, and the correct answer is
that labelled role. A model should do both: abstain when the question is ambiguous
and name the role when it is disambiguated. We measure both under a
\emph{per-condition} gold (ambiguous: gold $=$ unknown; disambiguated: gold $=$
the labelled role).

\textsc{SESGO}~\citep{robles2025sesgo} is a Spanish, Latin-American-grounded
benchmark in the BBQ ambiguous/disambiguated tradition, with items spanning the
categories racism, xenophobia, classism, and gender; we adopt it as our
benchmark. Our debiasing scaffolds instantiate the structure-aware
diversity-pursuit methodology of \citet{riossialer2026structureaware}.

\noindent\textbf{New in this work} (vs.\ prior: the \textsc{SESGO}
benchmark~\citep{robles2025sesgo} and the structure-aware diversity
method~\citep{riossialer2026structureaware}): a five-study scaffold-evaluation
framework; a multi-readout per-condition formalization combining non-thinking and
thinking, two-option and three-option, and greedy-thinking readouts; a behavioral
and representational comparison of the scaffold; and a cross-model size sweep.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Setup and notation}
\label{sec:notation}

Our primary subject is \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}, run locally; the cross-model study
(\Cref{sec:cross-model}) extends to four further models. The data are the
\textsc{SESGO} items~\citep{robles2025sesgo}; each item is rendered into a prompt
$x$ that poses a question and lists answer options realizing the \emph{roles}
\[
  y \in \{\, \mathrm{target}\;(t),\ \mathrm{other}\;(o),\ \mathrm{unknown}\;(u) \,\}.
\]
The gold role $y^\star$ is set \emph{per condition}: on \emph{ambiguous} items
$y^\star = u$ (abstain); on \emph{disambiguated} items $y^\star$ is the labelled
role ($t$ or $o$). A \emph{scaffold} $s$ is a one-sentence debiasing preamble; we
write $x \oplus s$ for the prompt with $s$ prepended and compare $\LLM(x)$ against
$\LLM(x \oplus s)$. Throughout, $\mathbf{1}[\cdot]$ is the indicator and
$\delta_u$ is the one-hot distribution placing all mass on $u$.

We read every item at \emph{three} levels: two teacher-forced readouts (over all
three roles, and over just the two named roles $t,o$ with the unknown option
removed) and a greedy reasoning decode; a sampled variant of the decode drives the
divergence study.

\paragraph{Teacher-forced readouts (three-option and two-option).}
We forbid reasoning (prefill a skip-thinking prefix and teacher-force each
option) and, for each role $y$, read the option-label token's conditional
log-probability at the answer position, $\ell_y = \log P(\text{label}_y \given
x)$, scored in one batched forward pass. The \emph{three-option} readout
renormalizes over all three roles,
\begin{equation}
  p^{\mathrm{nt}}_y \;=\; \frac{\exp(\ell_y)}{\sum_{y'} \exp(\ell_{y'})},
  \qquad y,y' \in \{t,o,u\},
  \label{eq:pnt}
\end{equation}
with chance $1/3$; the \emph{two-option} forced-choice readout drops the unknown
option and renormalizes over $\{t,o\}$ only (chance $1/2$), so it cannot abstain
and is undefined ($N/A$) on ambiguous items. Options are scored at \emph{display}
positions and remapped to roles through the item's position$\to$role permutation,
averaging out position bias. The prediction is $\hat{y} = \argmax_y p_y$ (ties
resolve to $u$ in the three-option case).

\paragraph{Greedy-thinking readout (decoded).}
We take a greedy reasoning decode (temperature $0$), strip the reasoning block,
and parse the committed role $r \in \{t,o,u,\varnothing\}$, where $\varnothing$
marks an undetectable or truncated draw (dropped); chance $1/3$. The
\textbf{divergence} study uses a \emph{sampled} variant: we draw $N$ generations
$g_i \sim \LLM(x)$, parse each to $r_i$, and over the $M \le N$ parsed draws form
\begin{equation}
  p^{\mathrm{th}}_y \;=\; \frac{1}{M}\sum_{i=1}^{M} \mathbf{1}[\, r_i = y \,],
  \label{eq:pth}
\end{equation}
with prediction $\hat{y}^{\mathrm{th}} = \argmax_y p^{\mathrm{th}}_y$ (ties resolve
to $u$).

\paragraph{Derived measures.}
The headline behavioral score is \emph{per-condition accuracy}---the fraction of
items whose prediction matches the condition's gold role $y^\star_q$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{acc} \;=\; \frac{1}{|\mathcal{Q}|}\sum_{q\in\mathcal{Q}}
    \mathbf{1}[\, \hat{y}_q = y^\star_q \,].
  \label{eq:acc}
\end{equation}
On ambiguous items ($y^\star_q = u$) this is \emph{abstention accuracy}; on
disambiguated items it is the rate of naming the correct labelled role, and the
$t$-vs-$o$ accuracy gap within disambiguated items is the bias signal. We report
Wilson 95\% confidence intervals on every rate. The three-option role masses
$(p^{\mathrm{nt}}_t,p^{\mathrm{nt}}_o,p^{\mathrm{nt}}_u)$ give a companion view of
where probability leaks. To characterize a reasoning distribution we use Shannon
entropy and divergence from correct abstention,
\begin{equation}
  H(p) = -\sum_{y} p_y \log p_y,
  \qquad
  \mathrm{JS}\!\left(p^{\mathrm{th}} \,\|\, \delta_u\right),
  \label{eq:entropy-js}
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{JS}$ is the Jensen--Shannon divergence (low when free-form reasoning
concentrates on $u$). For \emph{stability}, each item is rendered into $18$
cosmetic surface variants $\mathcal{V}_q$ ($3$ label styles $\times$ $6$ role
permutations, gold fixed); with $\mathrm{mode}_q$ the modal prediction, per-item
consistency is
\begin{equation}
  C_q \;=\; \frac{1}{|\mathcal{V}_q|}\sum_{v\in\mathcal{V}_q}
    \mathbf{1}[\, \hat{y}_v = \mathrm{mode}_q \,],
  \label{eq:stability}
\end{equation}
complemented by per-axis format-sensitivity (the share of single-axis variations
that change the prediction). Scaffold \emph{selection} picks
\begin{equation}
  s^\star \;=\; \argmax_s \; \mathrm{acc}(x \oplus s),
  \label{eq:select}
\end{equation}
the scaffold maximizing abstention accuracy over the candidate set.

\paragraph{Representational geometry.}
Along the greedy reasoning path we capture the residual-stream activation
$h^{(l)}_p(x) \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$ at every layer $l$ from mid to last ($14$--$27$)
and at \emph{eight} structural token positions $p \in \{\,\text{im\_end},\
\text{newline},\ \text{im\_start},\ \text{assistant},\ \text{think\_open},\
\text{think\_close},\ \text{answer\_prefix},\ \text{label}\,\}$, under scaffold and
no-scaffold conditions. At each layer and position we stack the per-sample vectors,
fit a (mean-centering) PCA, and colour points by each per-sample signal (context
condition, accuracy, gold label, question polarity, bias category, target/other
identity). For every such labelling we score the \emph{silhouette separability} in
the full PCA space: positive means same-label points cluster, near zero means no
structure, negative means same-label points are no closer than random.

\subsection{The debiasing scaffold}

The scaffold $s$ is a one-sentence preamble prepended to the question to push the
model toward the $u$ answer; we compare four bilingual scaffolds against the
no-scaffold baseline (\Cref{tab:scaffolds}). Each scaffold uses a distinct
mechanism and is authored in matched English and Spanish (EN/ES). All four derive
from the structure-aware diversity methodology of
\citet{riossialer2026structureaware}.

\textbf{baseline} reports per-condition accuracy (\Cref{eq:acc}) under all three
readouts and the role distribution $(p^{\mathrm{nt}}_t, p^{\mathrm{nt}}_o,

per-readout accuracy and $\mathrm{JS}(p^{\mathrm{th}}\|\delta_u)$
(\Cref{eq:entropy-js}). \textbf{geometry} captures answer-position activations
(scaffold vs.\ none) and reports the silhouette separability of the representation
by every item label.

\section{Results}

All numbers are run values, cross-checked against the raw
\texttt{response\_samples.json}. We report per-condition accuracy with Wilson
$95\%$ confidence intervals; $n$'s are study-specific and stated inline. The
baseline is the full SESGO grid on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} ($n{=}2310$ scored items:
$1155$ question\,ids $\times\,2$ context conditions, four bias categories).

\subsection{Baseline}
\label{sec:baseline}

\Cref{fig:baseline} reads the full grid under all three readouts, sliced by bias
category. On \emph{ambiguous} items (gold $=$ abstain) three-option teacher
forcing gives abstention accuracy $87\%$ clasismo ($n{=}136$, CI$[80,91]$),
$81\%$ racismo ($n{=}240$, CI$[76,86]$), $71\%$ xenofobia ($n{=}388$,
CI$[67,76]$), and $100\%$ genero ($n{=}6$). On \emph{disambiguated} items (gold
$=$ the labelled role) accuracy is lower and target-vs-other asymmetric. Under
three-option forcing, clasismo answers the target-gold role at $46\%$ vs.\
$35\%$ other (both $n{=}136$), racismo $28\%$ target vs.\ $43\%$ other ($n{=}240$),
and xenofobia $44\%$ vs.\ $40\%$ ($n{=}388$). The two-option forced choice (no
unknown option, chance $1/2$) gives racismo $50\%$ target vs.\ $64\%$ other and
xenofobia $58\%$ vs.\ $62\%$ ($n{=}240/388$). The role-probability mass
(\Cref{fig:baseline}(b)): unknown carries the plurality of mass in every category
(clasismo $0.536$, racismo $0.477$, xenofobia $0.432$, genero $0.412$), and the
named-role mass tilts toward \emph{other} over \emph{target} in racismo
($0.322$ vs.\ $0.202$) and genero ($0.362$ vs.\ $0.226$), and is near-symmetric in
clasismo ($0.220$ vs.\ $0.244$) and xenofobia ($0.295$ vs.\ $0.273$). Under
greedy-thinking, abstention is racismo $49\%$, xenofobia $45\%$, clasismo $85\%$.
The genero category has $n{=}18$ total.

\subsection{Cross-model size sweep}
\label{sec:cross-model}

Five instruction-tuned models---Qwen3 (0.6\,B, 4\,B), Llama-3.2 (1\,B, 3\,B), and
Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3---are placed against log model size, with $n{=}770$ per
metric cell per model (\Cref{fig:size-sweep}). Three-option abstention rises with
scale within each family: Qwen $0.774$ (0.6\,B) $\to 0.892$ (4\,B), Llama $0.299$
(1\,B) $\to 0.887$ (3\,B); greedy-thinking abstention rises likewise (Qwen
$0.534 \to 0.718$; Llama $0.275 \to 0.834$). \texttt{Llama-3.2-1B} abstains least
on the three-option readout ($0.299$, below the $1/3$ chance line) and has the
highest disambiguated-target rate ($0.678$ vs.\ $0.395$ for \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}).
On the two-option forced choice (no unknown option, abstention $0$ by
construction), average disambiguated accuracy is $0.596$ Qwen-0.6B and $0.587$
Llama-1B for the sub-1.3\,B models, and $0.822$ Llama-3B, $0.882$ Qwen-4B,
$0.835$ Mistral-7B for the 3--7\,B models.

\subsection{Stability}
\label{sec:stability}

\Cref{fig:stab-div}(a) reports robustness to surface variation ($18$ cosmetic
renderings per item: $3$ label styles $\times\,6$ role permutations, gold fixed;
single model, racismo, Spanish). Under the three-option readout, per-condition
accuracy is $44\%$ on ambiguous items ($63/144$, CI$[36,52]$) and $57\%$ on
disambiguated items ($163/288$, CI$[51,62]$); the two-option readout (N/A on
ambiguous items) reaches $60\%$ disambiguated ($174/288$, CI$[55,66]$). Per-item
consistency $C_q$ (\Cref{eq:stability}): ambiguous items keep their modal
three-option answer in $51\%\pm4\%$ of variations ($n{=}8$ items), disambiguated
items in $86\%\pm4\%$ ($n{=}16$). On the two-option choice, consistency is
ambiguous $83\%\pm6\%$, disambiguated $90\%\pm4\%$.

\subsection{Selection: the debiasing scaffold}
\label{sec:selection}

The four debiasing scaffolds of \Cref{tab:scaffolds} are defined as candidates, but
in this run the selection study scored only the no-scaffold baseline condition
(\texttt{scaffold\_id}\,$=$\,\texttt{(none)}) on $35$ ambiguous items (gold $=$
unknown), where per-condition accuracy is abstention accuracy
(\Cref{fig:stab-div}(b)). The selected scaffold $s^\star$ (gold star) is therefore
the no-scaffold baseline. Under $s^\star$, the three-option non-thinking readout
abstains on $80\%$ of items ($28/35$, CI$[64,90]$) and the sampled thinking
readout---on the $22$-item subset that produced parsable thinking draws---abstains
on $86\%$ ($19/22$, CI$[67,95]$). Within the thinking readout, abstention is
clasismo $100\%$ ($n{=}3$), racismo $100\%$ ($n{=}6$), and xenofobia $77\%$
($10/13$). The two-option forced choice abstains $0\%$ by construction
(target$=14$ vs.\ other$=21$). The scaffolds' \emph{representational} effect is
measured separately in the geometry study, where scaffold presence is the most
separable axis of the answer geometry (silhouette $+0.48$, \Cref{sec:geometry});
the behavioral per-scaffold abstention comparison is limited and pending.

\subsection{Divergence}
\label{sec:divergence}

Each prompt is scored at four readouts under the per-condition gold
(\Cref{fig:divergence-acc}). On the $14$ \emph{ambiguous} items (abstaining
correct): sampled thinking $86\%$ ($12/14$), three-option teacher forcing $79\%$
($11/14$), greedy-thinking $64\%$ ($9/14$); the two-option forced choice is
undefined (no unknown option). On the $29$ \emph{disambiguated} items (a role must
be named): the two-option forced choice $59\%$ ($17/29$), sampled thinking $48\%$
($14/29$), greedy-thinking $41\%$ ($12/29$), three-option readout $28\%$ ($8/29$).
On the ambiguous default mix the thinking draws are $84\%$ unknown, $9\%$ target,
$7\%$ other, and $11$ of $14$ items sit at $[0,0,1]$ (JS-divergence $0$). The mean
JS-divergence from $\delta_u$ is $0.092$; three items diverge (one onto \emph{other}
at $\mathrm{JS}=\ln 2 = 0.693$, two toward target at $0.216$ and $0.380$).

\subsection{Geometry}
\label{sec:geometry}

We read answer-position activations ($n{=}462$: $231$ scaffold $+$ $231$ no-scaffold
items) at every layer from mid to last ($14$--$27$) and at eight token positions
(\Cref{fig:geometry-sil}, \Cref{fig:geometry-axes}). At the answer-label token of the
last layer, silhouette separability is dominated by \emph{scaffold} presence ($+0.48$;
$+0.35$ to $+0.60$ across positions, separable at every layer), then
\emph{selected\_role} ($+0.20$, rising to $+0.36$ at mid-layer $21$); model accuracy
($+0.04$), \emph{context\_condition} ($+0.05$), gold\_role ($+0.05$) and
question\_polarity ($+0.01$) are near zero, while \emph{target\_identity} ($-0.47$) and
\emph{other\_identity} ($-0.48$) are anti-separable. The named conditions project onto
a single arc (PC1 $68\%$ EV, PC2 $9\%$ EV); the identity axes carry $152/154$ distinct
identity strings ($n{=}462$), so anti-separability reflects that nearly every point
has a unique identity label rather than any clustering by social group. The scaffold
preamble---not the social identity in the item---is what reshapes the answer geometry.

\section{Limitations and Dual-Use Considerations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Limitations.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{Single primary model.} The baseline, stability, selection,
    divergence, and geometry studies all run on one small model
    (\texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}); only the cross-model sweep spans five models (each
    family contributing two sizes), so the per-condition and geometric findings are
    not yet shown to generalize across models or scales.
  \item \textbf{Small per-cell $n$ on the pilot studies.} The stability, selection,
    and divergence studies use small item pools (stability $n{=}8/16$ items per
    condition; selection $35$ ambiguous items, of which $22$ produced parsable
    thinking draws; divergence $43$ scored items), so per-item Wilson CIs are wide
    and the slice-level rates (e.g.\ per-category abstention) should be read as
    pilot estimates rather than precise measurements.
  \item \textbf{Limited per-scaffold behavioral comparison.} The four candidate
    debiasing scaffolds in \Cref{tab:scaffolds} are fully defined, but in this run
    the selection study scored only the no-scaffold baseline condition, so the
    selected $s^\star$ is that baseline. The scaffolds' \emph{representational}
    effect is measured in the geometry study (scaffold-vs-none silhouette $+0.48$),
    but the full per-scaffold behavioral abstention comparison is still limited and
    pending.
  \item \textbf{Spanish-only scope.} Coverage is Spanish (with matched English
    scaffold text) and four bias categories (racism, xenophobia, classism, gender);
    the \textbf{genero} category is underpowered ($n{=}18$ total; $n{=}6$ per
    disambiguated slice), and findings may not transfer to other languages or
    social-bias taxonomies.
  \item \textbf{Correlational geometry.} The geometry study measures which item
    labels separate the answer representation; it is correlational, not causal, and
    does not establish that the scaffold axis \emph{drives} abstention behavior.
  \item \textbf{Parser dependence.} The greedy- and sampled-thinking readouts depend
    on a parser that can drop free-form draws ($4$ of $47$ divergence prompts and
    $13$ of $35$ selection items yielded no parsable thinking draw), which biases
    the parsed count $M$ and the thinking distribution $p^{\mathrm{th}}$.
  \item \textbf{Symmetry assumptions.} The position$\to$role remap and bilingual
    rendering assume the model treats display positions and the two languages
    symmetrically; residual position or language bias would leak into the reported
    rates.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Dual-use and misuse risks.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item The debiasing scaffold is invertible: the one-sentence preamble that pushes
    the model toward ``unknown'' can be rewritten into a bias-amplifying preamble
    that pushes it toward the stereotyped target group.
  \item Small models substitute confident stereotyped guessing for abstention, so a
    deployer choosing the cheapest model inherits the most biased behavior.
  \item Abstention can be gamed by answering ``unknown'' indiscriminately; the
    per-condition design scores indiscriminate abstention at $0$ on disambiguated
    items.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Suggestions for future improvements.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item Run all four \Cref{tab:scaffolds} scaffolds through the selection study, so
    the selected $s^\star$ rests on a real per-scaffold behavioral comparison rather
    than the no-scaffold baseline.
  \item Extend the per-condition and geometry studies across the cross-model sweep's
    models and sizes to test whether the findings generalize beyond \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}.
  \item Move the geometry from correlational to causal by steering on the scaffold
    axis and measuring the resulting change in abstention.
  \item Broaden coverage beyond Spanish and the four bias categories, and add items
    to the underpowered \textbf{genero} slice.
\end{itemize}
