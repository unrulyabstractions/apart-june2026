\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Parallel studies}
\label{sec:methods-studies}
Our primary subject is \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}, run locally; the cross-model study extends
to a sweep of open-weight models. Each \textsc{SESGO} item is rendered into a prompt
$x$ whose options realize the roles $y \in \{t\,(\text{target}),\, o\,(\text{other}),\,
u\,(\text{unknown})\}$, with a per-condition gold $y^\star$: on \emph{ambiguous} items
$y^\star{=}u$ (abstain), on \emph{disambiguated} items the labelled role. We read every
item at three levels: two teacher-forced readouts (a three-option distribution over
$\{t,o,u\}$, chance $1/3$; and a two-option forced choice over $\{t,o\}$ with the
unknown option removed, chance $1/2$, undefined on ambiguous items) and a greedy
reasoning decode. We score per-condition accuracy with Wilson $95\%$ CIs:
\begin{equation}
  p^{\mathrm{nt}}_y = \frac{\exp(\ell_y)}{\sum_{y'} \exp(\ell_{y'})},\quad y,y'\in\{t,o,u\};
  \qquad
  \mathrm{acc} = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{Q}|}\sum_{q\in\mathcal{Q}} \mathbf{1}[\,\hat{y}_q = y^\star_q\,].
  \label{eq:acc}
\end{equation}
We run three parallel studies (\Cref{tab:studies}), moving all further technicality to
the released harness.

\subsection{Reproducibility}
\label{sec:methods-repro}
We release the full evaluation harness (the three- and two-option choice runners, the
greedy- and sampled-thinking readouts, and the forking-paths analysis)
together with the \textsc{SESGO}-derived prompt data and per-model outputs on
HuggingFace (\texttt{unrulyabstractions/apart\_global\_south}).
