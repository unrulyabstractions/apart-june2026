\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We characterized bias in Spanish-prompted, open-weight LLMs along model scale, using
behavioral and distributional studies on the \textsc{SESGO} benchmark. Our
preliminary results suggest that smaller models are more biased, that they answer less
consistently, and that a thinking model resolves its answer at a few discrete forking tokens
rather than accreting it gradually. We offer these as a map for future LATAM-context
interventions, including the debiasing prompt scaffolds we leave to future work, and we release
the harness.
