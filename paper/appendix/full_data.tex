\section{Full-data cross-axis baseline}
\label{app:fulldata}

\paragraph{Methods.}
The full-data run scores the \textsc{SESGO} grid on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} across all
four scaffold conditions (\Cref{tab:scaffolds}: the no-scaffold baseline and the
three debiasing scaffolds) and both languages, reading every item under the three
readouts (3-opt, 2-opt, greedy-thinking). Coverage is
$16{,}164/24{,}480$ prompts ($66\%$), balanced across every axis: $4041$ samples
per scaffold, es $12{,}684$ / en $3480$, and original $10{,}320$ / bbq-adapted
$5844$. We report the three-option teacher-forced abstention on the $5388$
\emph{ambiguous} items (gold $=$ unknown), sliced by language, origin, and scaffold;
rates carry Wilson $95\%$ CIs. The per-scaffold slice drives scaffold selection
(\Cref{app:selection}).

\paragraph{Results.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{Language} (3-opt abstention, ambiguous): en $96.2\%$
    ($1116/1160$, CI$[95,97]$) vs.\ es $86.3\%$ ($3648/4228$, CI$[85,87]$)---the
    model abstains markedly more on the matched English rendering.
  \item \textbf{Origin}: bbq-adapted $96.1\%$ ($1872/1948$, CI$[95,97]$) vs.\
    original \textsc{SESGO} $84.1\%$ ($2892/3440$, CI$[83,85]$)---the
    BBQ-derived items elicit more abstention than the Latin-American-grounded
    original items.
  \item \textbf{Scaffold} (per condition, $n{=}1347$ each), in descending
    abstention: \texttt{interpretive\_direction} $99.9\%$;
    \texttt{prior\_dominance\_warning} $88.3\%$; \texttt{(none)} baseline $85.6\%$;
    \texttt{intent\_and\_register\_respect} $79.9\%$ (full numbers and CIs in
    \Cref{app:selection}).
  \item The cross-axis figure (\Cref{fig:selection}) shows all three slices side by
    side over the 3-opt, 2-opt, and greedy-thinking readouts; the 2-opt panel cannot
    abstain by construction.
\end{itemize}
