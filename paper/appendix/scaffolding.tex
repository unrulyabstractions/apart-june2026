\section{Scaffolding}
\label{app:scaffold}

This appendix establishes \textsc{Claim 3} (simple scaffolding could help de-bias
models): a full-data cross-axis run on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} selects the best
debiasing scaffold behaviorally, and a preliminary causal-steering note turns the
scaffold direction into an additive intervention.

\subsection*{C.1 Extended Methodology}

\paragraph{Full-data cross-axis run.}%
\label{app:fulldata}%
The full-data run scores the \textsc{SESGO} grid on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} across all
four scaffold conditions (\Cref{tab:scaffolds}: the no-scaffold baseline and the
three debiasing scaffolds) and both languages, reading every item under the three
readouts (3-opt, 2-opt, greedy-thinking). Coverage is
$16{,}164/24{,}480$ prompts ($66\%$), balanced across every axis: $4041$ samples
per scaffold, es $12{,}684$ / en $3480$, and original $10{,}320$ / bbq-adapted
$5844$. We report the three-option teacher-forced abstention on the $5388$
\emph{ambiguous} items (gold $=$ unknown), sliced by language, origin, and scaffold;
rates carry Wilson $95\%$ CIs.

\paragraph{Scaffold selection.}
The candidate scaffolds (\Cref{tab:scaffolds}) are scored and scaffold
\emph{selection} picks
\begin{equation}
  s^\star \;=\; \argmax_s \; \mathrm{acc}(x \oplus s),
  \label{eq:select}
\end{equation}
the scaffold maximizing abstention accuracy over the candidate set. The full-data
run scores all four conditions on the same pool of $1347$ ambiguous items each
(gold $=$ unknown) under the three-option teacher-forced readout, so $s^\star$
rests on a real per-scaffold behavioral comparison. The full bilingual scaffold
texts (English and Spanish) and the per-item prompt rendering are included in the
released harness; \Cref{tab:scaffolds} gives the one-line mechanism for each.

\paragraph{Causal steering (preliminary).}%
\label{app:steer}%
As a \emph{preliminary} causal probe, we turn the scaffold geometry
(\Cref{app:geometry}, where \emph{scaffold} is the most separable axis but only
correlationally) into an additive intervention. From the geometry residuals we
pair each item's scaffold and no-scaffold activation by question id and take the
per-layer diff-of-means, $v^{(l)} = \overline{h^{(l)}_{\text{scaffold}}} -
\overline{h^{(l)}_{\text{none}}}$, as a steering vector at the primary layer
$l{=}14$ (depth $0.50$). We split the $231$ pairs by question id into a seeded
$70/30$ train/test split (no item overlap) and fit nothing beyond the train-mean
direction; all steering numbers below are on the held-out test items. At decode we
add $\alpha\,v^{(14)}$ to the \texttt{resid\_post} stream of every token and
re-read the teacher-forced three-option abstention on the $n{=}24$ ambiguous test
items, sweeping $\alpha \in [-2, 4]$.

\subsection*{C.2 Extended Results}

\paragraph{Cross-axis full-data (\texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}, $n{=}5388$ ambiguous).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{Language} (3-opt abstention): en $96.2\%$ ($1116/1160$,
    CI$[95,97]$) vs.\ es $86.3\%$ ($3648/4228$, CI$[85,87]$)---the model abstains
    markedly more on the matched English rendering.
  \item \textbf{Origin}: bbq-adapted $96.1\%$ ($1872/1948$, CI$[95,97]$) vs.\
    original \textsc{SESGO} $84.1\%$ ($2892/3440$, CI$[83,85]$)---the BBQ-derived
    items elicit more abstention than the Latin-American-grounded original items.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Scaffold selection (per condition, $n{=}1347$ each).}
In descending three-option abstention:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \texttt{interpretive\_direction}: $99.9\%$ ($1345/1347$, CI$[99,100]$)
    ---near-total abstention; this is the selected $s^\star$.
  \item \texttt{prior\_dominance\_warning}: $88.3\%$ ($1190/1347$, CI$[87,90]$).
  \item \texttt{(none)} no-scaffold baseline: $85.6\%$ ($1153/1347$, CI$[84,87]$).
  \item \texttt{intent\_and\_register\_respect}: $79.9\%$ ($1076/1347$,
    CI$[78,82]$)---this scaffold \emph{underperforms the no-scaffold baseline}, so
    not all scaffolds help; scaffold choice matters.
  \item The selected $s^\star{=}$\texttt{interpretive\_direction} lifts abstention
    $+14.3$ points over the no-scaffold baseline ($99.9\%$ vs.\ $85.6\%$); the two
    intermediate scaffolds bracket the baseline (one above, one below). This backs
    the central contribution behaviorally and corroborates the representational
    finding (scaffold-vs-none silhouette $+0.48$, \Cref{app:geometry}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Causal steering (preliminary; held-out $n{=}24$).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item Adding the scaffold axis \emph{causally raises abstention} on held-out
    ambiguous items: test abstention climbs from $0.54$ at $\alpha{=}0$ to $0.71$
    at $\alpha{=}{+}1$ and a peak $0.75$ at $\alpha{=}{+}2$ (unknown-mass
    $0.49 \to 0.62$).
  \item The negative direction \emph{suppresses} it: $\alpha{=}{-}2$ drops
    abstention to $0.17$ (unknown-mass $0.18$), confirming a signed, monotone axis
    rather than a generic noise perturbation.
  \item The effect is bounded: over-steering at $\alpha{=}{+}4$ collapses back to
    the $\alpha{=}0$ baseline ($0.54$). Reference ceiling: the actual scaffold
    prompt reaches $1.00$ abstention ($0.99$ unknown-mass) on the same test items,
    so a single mid-layer diff-of-means vector recovers a substantial fraction of
    the scaffold's behavioral effect with no prompt change. This single-model
    result is \emph{preliminary}.
\end{itemize}
