\section{Model Scale}
\label{app:scale}

This appendix replicates the \textsc{SESGO} baseline on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B} and
extends it into a $12$-model size sweep across four families, establishing
\textsc{Claim 1} (smaller models are more biased) behaviorally.

\subsection*{A.1 Extended Methodology}

\paragraph{Baseline grid.}
The baseline is the full \textsc{SESGO} grid on \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}
($n{=}2310$ scored items: $1155$ question\,ids $\times\,2$ context conditions,
four bias categories), with no scaffold and no surface variation. It reads every
item under all three readouts (3-opt, 2-opt, greedy-thinking) and reports
per-condition accuracy (\Cref{eq:acc}) and the three-option role distribution
$(p^{\mathrm{nt}}_t, p^{\mathrm{nt}}_o, p^{\mathrm{nt}}_u)$, sliced by bias
category. All numbers are run values, cross-checked against the raw
\texttt{response\_samples.json}; rates carry Wilson $95\%$ CIs.

\paragraph{Cross-model size sweep.}
The cross-model study repeats the baseline readouts across instruction-tuned
models spanning four families---Qwen3 (0.6\,B, 4\,B, 14\,B, 32\,B), Llama-3.2
(1\,B, 3\,B) and Llama-3.1 (70\,B), Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, and Gemma-2
(including \texttt{gemma-2-27b-it})---and plots accuracy against log model size,
with $n{=}770$ per metric cell per model. No scaffold and no surface variation are
applied. The bias score combines accuracy and the signed error split into the
single magnitude
\begin{equation}
  \textrm{bias\_score} \;=\; \sigma \cdot \sqrt{(1-\mathrm{acc})^2 +
    \big(F(\mathrm{Target}) - F(\mathrm{Other})\big)^2},
  \label{eq:bias-score}
\end{equation}
where $F(\mathrm{Target})$ and $F(\mathrm{Other})$ are the shares of
\emph{incorrect} answers naming the marginalized target vs.\ the other group, and
their signed difference (the \emph{bias alignment}) is plotted per model in
\Cref{fig:bias-alignment-baseline}.

\subsection*{A.2 Extended Results}

\paragraph{Baseline (\texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}, $n{=}2310$).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item Ambiguous, three-option abstention accuracy: clasismo $87\%$ ($n{=}136$,
    CI$[80,91]$); racismo $81\%$ ($n{=}240$, CI$[76,86]$); xenofobia $71\%$
    ($n{=}388$, CI$[67,76]$); genero $100\%$ ($n{=}6$).
  \item Disambiguated, three-option (target vs.\ other): clasismo $46\%$ vs.\
    $35\%$ (both $n{=}136$); racismo $28\%$ vs.\ $43\%$ ($n{=}240$); xenofobia
    $44\%$ vs.\ $40\%$ ($n{=}388$).
  \item Disambiguated, two-option forced choice (chance $1/2$, target vs.\ other):
    racismo $50\%$ vs.\ $64\%$ ($n{=}240$); xenofobia $58\%$ vs.\ $62\%$
    ($n{=}388$).
  \item Three-option role mass, unknown share: clasismo $0.536$; racismo $0.477$;
    xenofobia $0.432$; genero $0.412$ (plurality in every category).
  \item Named-role mass (target vs.\ other): racismo $0.202$ vs.\ $0.322$; genero
    $0.226$ vs.\ $0.362$; clasismo $0.244$ vs.\ $0.220$; xenofobia $0.273$ vs.\
    $0.295$ (tilts to \emph{other} in racismo/genero, near-symmetric otherwise).
  \item Greedy-thinking abstention: racismo $49\%$; xenofobia $45\%$; clasismo
    $85\%$.
  \item genero category: $n{=}18$ total.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Cross-model size sweep ($n{=}770$ per cell).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item Three-option abstention rises with scale within each family: Qwen $0.774$
    (0.6\,B) $\to 0.892$ (4\,B); Llama $0.299$ (1\,B) $\to 0.887$ (3\,B).
  \item Greedy-thinking abstention rises likewise: Qwen $0.534 \to 0.718$; Llama
    $0.275 \to 0.834$.
  \item \texttt{Llama-3.2-1B} abstains least on the three-option readout ($0.299$,
    below the $1/3$ chance line) and has the highest disambiguated-target rate
    ($0.678$ vs.\ $0.395$ for \texttt{Qwen3-0.6B}).
  \item Two-option disambiguated accuracy (abstention $0$ by construction),
    sub-1.3\,B: Qwen-0.6B $0.596$, Llama-1B $0.587$.
  \item Two-option disambiguated accuracy, 3--7\,B: Llama-3B $0.822$, Qwen-4B
    $0.882$, Mistral-7B $0.835$.
  \item Bias alignment (\Cref{eq:bias-score}) narrows with scale: small models are
    both less accurate and more biased (e.g.\ \texttt{Llama-3.2-1B} ambiguous acc
    $0.13$, bias\_score $0.87$, widest and most biased), while large Qwen
    (14\,B acc $0.93$, 32\,B acc $0.95$) sit high and narrow near zero bias.
  \item \texttt{gemma-2-27b-it} is a degenerate run (disambiguated accuracy
    $\approx0$) and is shown honestly rather than dropped.
\end{itemize}
